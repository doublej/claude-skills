# Banned Words and Phrases

Replace with plain language or delete entirely.

## Corporate Buzzwords
- leverage (as verb)
- synergy / synergize
- comprehensive
- robust
- holistic
- streamline
- empower
- facilitate
- utilize (just say "use")
- optimize (unless actually optimizing code)
- circle back
- align / alignment
- bandwidth (meaning availability)
- deep-dive
- pivot

## AI Slop Words
- delve
- unlock
- unleash
- harness
- transformative
- cutting-edge
- groundbreaking
- revolutionize
- elevate
- foster
- seamless / seamlessly
- supercharge
- multifaceted
- nuanced (as filler)

## Dead Phrases — Email
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "Please do not hesitate to reach out"
- "At your earliest convenience"
- "As per my last email"
- "Just wanted to check in"
- "Just wanted to follow up"
- "Just wanted to reach out"
- "Per our conversation"
- "Going forward"
- "Kindly" (just say "please")
- "Regards" / "Warm regards" / "Kind regards"
- "I'm thrilled to share"
- "Excited to announce"

## Dead Phrases — Messaging
- "Hope you're doing well!"
- "Sorry to bother you but..."
- "No worries if not!"
- "Just a quick one"
- "Quick question" (just ask the question)
- "Does that make sense?" (rephrase as specific question)
- "Let me know your thoughts" (ask something specific)

## AI Giveaway Punctuation
- em-dash (—) — never use. Rewrite with comma, period, or "and"
- semicolons in casual messages — just use two sentences
- ellipsis for dramatic effect ("and then... it worked") — cut it

## AI Giveaway Sentence Starters
- "Interestingly,"
- "Importantly,"
- "Notably,"
- "Crucially,"
- "Furthermore,"
- "Moreover,"
- "Additionally,"
- "It's worth noting that"
- "It's important to note that"

## AI Giveaway Structures
- "Not only X, but also Y"
- "Whether it's X or Y, ..."
- "While X, Y" (overused as opener)
- "This means..." / "What this means is..."
- "In other words,"
- "That said,"
- "That being said,"
- "With that in mind,"
- Lists of exactly three adjectives ("fast, reliable, and scalable")
- Mirrored sentence pairs ("X is great. But Y is even better.")

## AI Giveaway Tone
- False enthusiasm not present in source text
- "I completely understand..."
- "Great question!" / "That's a great point!"
- Summarizing what someone just said back to them
- Overly smooth transitions between unrelated points

## What to Do Instead
- Say what you mean directly
- Ask the actual question
- State the actual deadline
- Use specific, concrete language
- When in doubt, shorter is better
- Read it out loud — if it sounds like a LinkedIn post, rewrite it
